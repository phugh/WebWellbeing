Welcome

Web Wellbeing is for entertainment purposes only!


Web Wellbeing is here to help you make more informed choices about the type of content you view and to be more mindful about the websites you visit.


Web Wellbeing rates the pages you view based on the PERMA model of wellbeing. PERMA stands for Positive emotion, Engagement, Positive Relationships, Meaning, and Accomplishment. These are the five components of psychological wellbeing. Each has a negative counterpart: negative emotion, disengagement, negative relationships, lack of meaning, and lack of accomplishment.


Web Wellbeing scans each element on a page and assigns it a wellbeing rating based on the PERMA components above. If 3/5 negative components score higher than their positive counterparts the element will be redacted or the page will be blocked depending on what action and threshold you select below.

The lexical data that Web Wellbeing uses contains contiguous chunks of text, two and three chunks long, called bigrams and trigrams respectively. Including bigrams and trigrams makes the analysis more accurate but triples the processing power required.


References

The PERMA model of wellbeing comes primarily from the work of Dr. Martin Seligman. See the 2011 book "Flourish" for more info.


The wellbeing assignment code is based on Schwartz, H.A., Sap, M., Kern, M.L., Eichstaedt, J.C., Kapelner, A., Agrawal, M., Blanco, E., Dziurzynski, L., Park, G., Stillwell, D. & Kosinski, M. (2016). Predicting individual well-being through the language of social media. In Biocomputing 2016: Proceedings of the Pacific Symposium (pp. 516-527).
